Getting started

Install SwiftSale, launch the app, and get your workspace ready before your live show begins.

Setup

What to do first

  1. Install SwiftSale and open the app on your show computer.
  2. Make sure your workspace is clean and you can see the Capture, Games, Shipping, and Buyer History areas.
  3. Confirm your preferred output folders and file locations before going live.
  4. Test your workflow once before the show starts so everything feels familiar.

What SwiftSale is built for

SwiftSale is designed to help live sellers move faster after every sale. It helps you capture winners, assign bins, run games, annotate labels, manage mailing data, and keep track of repeat buyers in one clean workflow.

02

Starting a show

Begin each session with a clean show context so SwiftSale can organize everything correctly.

Show Control

How it works

  • Start your show session before you begin capturing winners.
  • This gives SwiftSale a clear active show context for entries, game activity, and history.
  • Keeping each session separate makes your data cleaner and easier to review later.
Best practice

Start a fresh show session at the beginning of every live event. That keeps your current activity, game entries, and exported data tied to the right show.

03

Capturing winners

Capture each winning buyer as the show moves so your post-show workflow stays organized in real time.

Core Workflow

Capture flow

  1. As each winner is identified, capture their username into SwiftSale.
  2. SwiftSale records the buyer as part of your active show workflow.
  3. That buyer can then be used for bin assignment, shipping preparation, and history tracking.

Why this matters

The capture step is the foundation of the whole system. Once a winner is captured, SwiftSale can keep that buyer connected to their assigned bin, game entries, mailing information, and buyer history across the rest of the workflow.

04

Assigning bins

Bin assignment keeps each buyer organized so fulfillment is faster and less error-prone.

Organization

Bin workflow

  • After a buyer is captured, assign them a bin.
  • SwiftSale keeps that assignment connected to the buyer for the show.
  • Clear bin organization makes picking, packing, and checking orders easier later.

Operational advantage

Instead of relying on memory or scattered notes, SwiftSale gives you a structured way to connect the buyer with a physical holding location. That means fewer mistakes and faster shipping prep after the show ends.

Turn normal show participation into an engaging live game that keeps buyers watching and interacting.

Games

What Bingo does

SwiftSale’s Bingo feature lets you build live-show excitement around participation and entries. It gives you a game layer on top of your normal selling process so buyers feel more involved.

How to use it

  1. Open the Bingo area from your games workflow.
  2. Load or confirm your player entries.
  3. Start the game during your show when you want to add excitement.
  4. Use it as a reward moment, giveaway layer, or engagement boost.
06

Using Bin Bonanza

Add a premium game-show moment by selecting from your assigned bins in a dramatic visual format.

Live Energy

What Bin Bonanza is for

Bin Bonanza transforms your assigned bins into an audience-facing game moment. Instead of a plain random selection, you get a more visual, entertaining, game-show style experience tied directly to the live workflow.

How to run it well

  • Use Bin Bonanza after bins have been assigned so the game feels connected to the show.
  • Present it during a hype moment when engagement is already high.
  • Keep the prize clear so viewers immediately understand what is at stake.
  • Use it as a branded show segment, not just a random utility tool.
07

Shipping / label annotation

Move from live selling into fulfillment with label tools that help connect buyers, bins, and shipping documents.

Fulfillment

Shipping workflow overview

  1. Open the Shipping area once you are ready to process labels.
  2. Select your label files and required data sources.
  3. Run annotation so labels can include the useful workflow details you need.
  4. Save the processed output for packing and fulfillment.

Why annotation helps

Label annotation helps bridge the gap between the buyer you captured during the show and the shipping paperwork you need afterward. This makes packing faster, clearer, and easier to verify.

09

Buyer history

Track repeat buyers and understand buyer activity over time instead of treating every show like a blank slate.

Insights

Why buyer history matters

Buyer history helps you recognize loyal customers, understand repeat activity, and keep a clearer picture of who is actively buying from you. That creates a more informed selling workflow and helps you treat repeat customers like repeat customers.

How to think about it

  • Each captured winner contributes to your buyer records.
  • Over time, this creates a more useful history of who buys from your shows.
  • That history can improve recognition, service, and long-term workflow decisions.
10

Tips / best practices

A few habits can make SwiftSale feel even smoother during fast-moving live shows.

Pro Tips

Keep your workflow consistent

  • Start a fresh show session every time you go live.
  • Capture winners immediately instead of waiting.
  • Assign bins as you go so shipping is easier later.

Use games intentionally

  • Run Bingo when you want sustained engagement.
  • Use Bin Bonanza for a more dramatic spotlight moment.
  • Always make the reward clear before the game starts.

Prepare for shipping early

  • Do not wait until the end to think about fulfillment.
  • Organized bins make annotation and packing easier.
  • Keep your file locations predictable and clean.

Treat the show like a system

  • SwiftSale works best when your process is repeatable.
  • Use the same rhythm from show to show.
  • Small consistency improvements compound over time.
11

Troubleshooting

When something feels off, a few quick checks usually solve the issue faster than guessing.

Support

Show workflow issues

  • Make sure you started an active show session.
  • Confirm you are working in the correct section of the app.
  • Refresh your workflow from the current show context first.

Capture or bin issues

  • Check that winners are being captured in the right flow.
  • Confirm the buyer was recorded before expecting downstream actions.
  • Review your process order: capture first, assign second.

Shipping or export issues

  • Verify the correct files are selected before processing.
  • Keep source files and output folders easy to find.
  • Run a small test when you change your workflow.

Game workflow issues

  • Make sure the correct entries or bin data exist before launching the game.
  • Use game features after your core workflow is populated.
  • If a show segment feels confusing, simplify the sequence and try again.
